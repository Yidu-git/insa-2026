## What is Blue Teaming
___
**Blue teaming** (in *cybersecurity*) is a proactive defense approach focused on maintaining and updating cybersecurity infrastructures. Its usually done while identifying vulnerabilities, and mitigating active security threats. Unlike *Red teaming* and other attacking frameworks that focus on simulating attacks, blue teaming focuses on responding to the attacks simulated by red teamers.

### Core principles of blue teaming

The core of blue teaming relies on the fundamentals of the **defense-in-depth** strategy, constant vigilance and operational resilience/ This includes implementing the principle of least privilege to restrict user access.

## Types of logs
---
In Blue teaming, logs are essential to monitor traffic and record attacks. Logs are categorized by their origin and the type of data they record:
- **Authentication Logs:** Track user login attempts, logouts, privilege escalations, and failed password entries to spot unauthorized access.
- **Application Logs:** Recorded by specific software programs (like databases or web servers) to track user actions, errors, and system events.
- **System Logs:** Generated by the operating system (Windows Event Logs, Linux Syslog) to record hardware changes, reboots, and driver activities.
- **Network Logs:** Created by routers and switches to map traffic flow, ports used, and connection durations between IP addresses.
- **Security Logs:** Produced by security tools (Firewalls, IDS/IPS, Antivirus) to flag blocked traffic, malware detections, and policy violations.
- **API Logs:** Record requests and responses between software services, tracking data exchanges and integration requests.
- **Audit Logs:** Chronological records providing documentary evidence of sequence of activities, crucial for regulatory compliance.
